Working papers and ongoing projects

Fiscal Union Without Factor Union: Optimal VAT under Monopolistic Competition

Nicolas Djob Li Ngue Bikob · 2026 · Working paper

VAT · Optimal taxation

This paper studies destination-based value-added taxation in a two-country economy with monopolistic competition, endogenous entry and asymmetric public-expenditure requirements. A supranational planner can accommodate international imbalances through a zero-sum transfer, creating a common marginal cost of public funds. The optimal Ramsey rule combines social resource costs, consumption values, product-market competition and entry responses.

Commodity Tax Principles, Firm Location, and Skilled-Wage Incidence under the Destination and Origin Principles

Nicolas Djob Li Ngue Bikob · 2026 · Working paper

Tax competition

The paper compares destination- and origin-based commodity taxation in an economy with monopolistic competition, mobile firms and heterogeneous labour. Destination taxation changes consumer prices but leaves production location pinned down by factor supplies. Origin taxation changes the relative attractiveness of locations and therefore affects skilled wages, capital income, consumer prices and fiscal revenue through firm relocation.

Monopolistic Competition in OLG Models: Efficiency and Inequality

Pascal Belan and Nicolas Djob Li Ngue Bikob · 2026 · Work in progress

OLG · Market power

This project studies how firms’ market power affects capital accumulation and the distribution of income in an overlapping-generations economy. Markups redistribute income between retirees, who supply capital, and young skilled and unskilled workers. The analysis characterizes policies that correct productive inefficiencies while addressing intergenerational and intragenerational redistribution.

Commodity Tax Principles, Iceberg Trade Costs, and Skilled-Wage Incidence

Anderson Vil, Nicolas Djob Li Ngue Bikob and Usama Jamal · 2026 · Work in progress

International taxation

This project studies the design and incidence of commodity taxation under the destination and origin principles when international trade is subject to iceberg costs. The analysis examines how trade frictions affect firms’ location decisions, cross-border tax competition, consumer prices, and skilled wages. Particular attention is paid to the interaction between commodity-tax rules, market access, production relocation, and the distribution of welfare between skilled workers, unskilled workers, consumers, and governments.

Taxation in a Globalized Economy: Resource Allocation, Monopolistic Competition, and Cross-Border Dynamics

Nicolas Djob Li Ngue Bikob · CY Cergy Paris Université · 2025

PhD thesis

The dissertation studies tax-policy design in a globalized economy. Its chapters examine optimal destination-based VAT, the welfare comparison between destination and origin principles, and extensions involving trade costs and the mobility of skilled labour.
